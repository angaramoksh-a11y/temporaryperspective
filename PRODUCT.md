# Temporary Perspective

## Register

brand

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Temporary Perspective (TP), a B2B podcast production studio in
Mumbai. The site sells a high-trust, done-for-you service to founders and brand
leads. Its job: route serious buyers into a 30-min call, and prove craft through
the work itself. The site IS the studio's first impression; production quality on
the page must mirror production quality of the shows.

## Users

- B2B founders (fintech, SaaS, finance, policy) who want a show worth taking seriously.
- Brand / content heads evaluating a production partner.
- They are time-poor, allergic to agency fluff, and judge on proof, not promises.

## Brand voice

Three words: **calm, sculptural, serious.** Confident understatement. The studio is
credited but never in front of the client. Copy is plain and direct, no hype, no
exclamation. "The show is always the client's. We're the studio behind it."

- No em dashes. No hype adjectives. Let the work and the guest names carry weight.
- Lowercase, quiet metadata. Big statements in serif.

## Anti-references

- Generic agency landing page (gradient hero, stat counters, logo soup).
- YouTube-playlist energy: bright thumbnails screaming for clicks.
- Editorial-magazine cliche (drop caps, broadsheet grid) without reason.

## Strategic principles

- Proof over claims. Recognizable guests (Vikram Sood, Saurabh Mukherjea, Manish
  Sabharwal) and named clients (Bharatvaarta, Bureau, Qapita) do the persuading.
- One dominant idea per fold. Negative space is a feature, not waste.
- Every page ends in the same quiet CTA: "Let's talk about your show." → book a call.
- The studio is the frame, not the subject. Restraint is the brand.

## Reference

Resend.com: near-black canvas, a single sculptural focal object, calm motion,
chromium edge-light dividers, premium dev-tool chrome. Adopt the stance, not the
literal layout.
